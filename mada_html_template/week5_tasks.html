Task 2: Evaluate Resources

Evaluation of WHO Website

  • ACCURACY: The WHO website provides accurate and evidence-based information on global health issues. Information is supported by research and citations.
  • ACTIVE VOICE: Active voice is used effectively to convey information directly and clearly. For example, "WHO promotes health, keeps the world safe, and serves the vulnerable."
  • LIMIT BIASES: The website avoids gender biases by using inclusive language. It strives to present health information without cultural or gender stereotypes.
  • CLARITY: Technical terms are explained in accessible language. Jargon is minimal, and definitions are provided where necessary.
  • CONCISENESS: Information is presented succinctly, without unnecessary elaboration. Key points are highlighted for quick understanding.
  • CONVERSATIONAL AND SMOOTHLY FLOWING PROSE: The writing style is clear and engaging, making complex health issues understandable to a broad audience.
  • CORRECTNESS: Grammar and spelling are consistently correct throughout the website. The language is professional and authoritative.
  • PARALLELISM: Parallel structure is used effectively, especially in lists and key messages. This enhances readability and clarity.
  • POSITIVE VOICE: Information is framed positively to empower individuals and communities toward better health outcomes.
  • STRONG NOUNS AND VERBS: Strong nouns and verbs are used to convey important health messages clearly and effectively.
  • SENTENCE VARIETY: There is a good mix of sentence lengths and structures, keeping the reader engaged.
  • SIMPLE WORDS: Complex medical terms are explained in simple terms, ensuring accessibility for all users.
  • SHORTER PARAGRAPHS: Paragraphs are kept relatively short, enhancing readability and encouraging users to digest information easily.
  • TONE AND VOICE: The tone is professional, yet empathetic and supportive, reflecting WHO's mission to promote health equity worldwide.

Voyant Tools Analysis and Comparison

Voyant Tools Analysis

The Voyant Tools analysis provided several insights into the WHO website's content:

  • Word Cloud (Cirrus): The most frequent words include "health," "news," "2024," "global," "Africa," "data," and "July." This indicates a focus on recent health updates, global health issues, and specific attention to Africa and July 2024.
  • Trends Graph: The graph shows the prominence of key terms over different segments of the document. "2024" peaks towards the end, indicating future-oriented information, while "health" remains consistent throughout, emphasizing it as the central theme.

Comparison with Professional Writing Characteristics

By comparing the findings from Voyant Tools with the professional writing characteristics, we can draw several conclusions:

  • Accuracy: The frequent use of specific terms like "data" and "health" supports the accuracy and evidence-based nature of the content.
  • Clarity and Conciseness: The word cloud and trends analysis show a clear focus on key topics, indicating that the content is likely concise and to the point.
  • Engaging Prose: The consistent mention of relevant and timely topics like "news" and "2024" suggests that the content is engaging and up-to-date.
  • Inclusivity and Bias Limitation: The absence of culturally or gender-biased terms in the word cloud supports the inclusivity of the language used.

Overall, the Voyant Tools analysis confirms that the WHO website adheres to many of the professional writing characteristics, particularly in terms of accuracy, clarity, and engagement.

Sample Paragraphs

Opening Statement: In today's digital age, protecting your personal information online is more important than ever. Our website is dedicated to providing young people with the knowledge and tools they need to stay safe in the digital world. From understanding the basics of online privacy to advanced tips on avoiding cyber threats, we've got you covered.

Contextualization: Navigating the internet can be overwhelming, especially with the constant threat of cyber attacks. Our goal is to make digital security accessible and understandable for everyone. We break down complex topics into easy-to-understand guides and provide practical advice to help you protect your online identity. Stay informed, stay secure.

Key Argument: Cybersecurity is not just the responsibility of tech experts; it's something we all need to be aware of. By taking simple steps, such as using strong passwords and being cautious about the information you share online, you can significantly reduce your risk of falling victim to cybercrime. Our website empowers you with the knowledge to make informed decisions and safeguard your digital life.
