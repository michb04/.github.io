Task 1: Identifying Your Audience(s)

Based on the topic of digital security and data privacy, the primary audience segments have been identified as:

Audience Segment Demographic Descriptors Characteristics Needs
Young Adults Age: 18-25 Tech-savvy, digitally active Practical cybersecurity tips, understanding privacy laws
Parents Age: 30-45 Concerned about family safety online Simple, effective cybersecurity guidance for family protection
Small Business Owners Age: 25-50 Managing digital assets, limited IT support Affordable and efficient cybersecurity solutions
Seniors Age: 65+ Limited tech knowledge, vulnerability to scams Basic digital security education and protection

Task 2: Building Audience Personas

Based on the identified audience segments, personas have been created to guide design decisions:

Persona: Emma Young

  • Age: 22
  • Occupation: College Student
  • Background: Tech-savvy, active on social media
  • Needs: Practical tips on digital security, understanding privacy laws

Emma is a college student studying digital media. She spends a lot of time online for both studies and social interactions. She is aware of the risks but often unsure about how to protect herself effectively. Emma is looking for clear, concise information on digital security and data privacy that she can easily apply in her daily life.

Persona: Mark Johnson

  • Age: 38
  • Occupation: Small Business Owner
  • Background: Managing a small tech business
  • Needs: Affordable and efficient cybersecurity solutions

Mark owns a small tech startup and is responsible for managing digital assets and client data. With limited IT support, he seeks cost-effective cybersecurity solutions that can safeguard his business against potential threats.

Task 3: Exploring Audience Needs in Relation to Design

Methods to explore audience needs include:

  • User Journey Mapping: Understand how different audience personas interact with digital security information.
  • User Stories: Write narratives from the perspective of each persona to outline specific design needs.
  • User Need Statements: Define concise statements that capture the core needs of each audience segment.

These methods will inform the design of multimedia content aimed at addressing the specific needs and expectations of each audience segment.
